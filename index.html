About this tool

A serverless dashboard for L1 supply & flow on Osmosis. Everything runs in your browser — no backend, no keys. It reads the chains directly from public endpoints.

The overhang thesis: most bridged L1 sits in wallets, not in the pools. That supply is latent — unpriced until someone sells it. This tool surfaces what's flowing now, what's sitting latent, and what's about to unlock.

What each box means
Market stats (top)
Live L1 price, 24h change, volume, and market cap (price × on-chain total supply).
Net pressure
L1 sold vs bought over the chosen window. Every swap is counted — arbitrage bots that buy and sell the same L1 cancel out on their own, so the net reflects real directional pressure.
Sell / buy ratio
Balance of selling vs buying, plus unique sellers vs buyers.
Top wallet concentration
Share of all selling coming from a single wallet — flags when one actor drives a move.
Price spread · slippage
Traded USD price range (p10–p90 of swaps ≥100 L1), a proxy for how much the thin pool moves per trade.
🛢 Overhang
Total L1 on Osmosis minus pool reserves: the latent wallet-held supply, as an absolute, a % of all L1 on Osmosis, and a multiple of pool liquidity.
Holder concentration
Top 1/3/5/10/20 share of total, the 15 largest holders, and a configurable "wallets holding ≥ X L1" histogram. Full live holder set.
🎯 Top sellers
Sellers over the window, aggregated per wallet.
⚠ Watchlist selling
Watchlisted wallets that are selling. The watchlist auto-adds receivers of large inbound IBC transfers — freshly bridged supply is the likeliest to sell next.
🟡 IBC out → Osmosis
Outbound L1 from GenesisL1, decoded from the packet. A leading signal of supply heading to market.
🟢 IBC in ← Osmosis
L1 bridging back to GenesisL1 — the counterweight.
📅 Unstaking calendar
Each unbonding as its own row (amount, delegator, USD, completion, days left), sorted by completion. ≥50k unlocks flagged.
🔴 Sells / 🟢 Buys
The raw, timestamped swap feed behind the net-pressure number.
💧 Pool depth
Price impact of a 1,000 L1 sell per pool.
🔀 Multi-hop audit
Transactions where L1 was both bought and sold (routing/arbitrage). Shown for transparency — both legs are counted in net pressure, so they cancel out naturally rather than being excluded.
